Chemistry Passage: Reaction Rates

Chemical reactions occur when particles collide with enough energy and in the correct orientation. The rate of a reaction can be increased in several ways. Raising the temperature makes particles move faster, producing more frequent and energetic collisions. Increasing concentration places more particles in the same volume, so collisions occur more often. Increasing surface area exposes more particles to possible collisions. A catalyst speeds a reaction by providing an alternative pathway with a lower activation energy. The catalyst participates in the process but is not consumed by the overall reaction.
