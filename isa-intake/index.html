Open Isa’s Intake Form
0–5 minWelcome and Framing

Purpose

Build trust, reduce the feeling of being evaluated, and identify what Isa wants from the work.

Suggested framing: “Today is about figuring out what already works, where things get harder, and what kind of system would actually make your life easier. This is not a test, and I am not expecting perfect answers.”

Use the Start tab

  • What would make these sessions worthwhile?
  • What would you most like to be easier when school starts?
  • What are you already good at managing?
Capture her exact wording. Her words become the student-owned goals and provide language for future progress reporting.
5–20 minFull Brain Dump

Use the Brain Dump tab

Capture everything Isa wants or needs to manage before August 12. Do not organize it perfectly yet.

  • Academics, SAT preparation, and Mandarin
  • Sports and extracurricular activities
  • Friends, calls, events, and social time
  • Gaming, music, art, and personal goals
  • Family commitments, health, sleep, and routines
  • School supplies, schedule preparation, and subject review

For each item

  • Deadline or frequency
  • Estimated time
  • Importance
  • Whether it has been started
  • Possible next action
Observe: memory load, deadline awareness, information stored in multiple places, realism of time estimates, prioritization, and signs of overwhelm. Let her thinking remain visible before helping organize it.
20–32 minCurrent-System Interview

Use your coach workbook for notes. This portion should feel conversational rather than like a survey.

Assignment capture

  • How did you know what homework you had?
  • Where did teachers post assignments?
  • How often did something surprise you?
  • How did you check for missing work?

Planning and starting

  • How did you decide what to work on after school?
  • Did you plan the week or handle each day as it came?
  • What happened when several things were due?
  • What happens between deciding to work and actually starting?

Studying and chemistry

  • When you say you studied, what did you actually do?
  • How did you prepare for the five-week Honors Chemistry course?
  • What worked, and what took too much time?
  • How did you know whether you really understood something?

Focus and independence

  • What distracted you most?
  • What happened when you felt behind or confused?
  • Who usually noticed problems first?
  • How comfortable are you asking a teacher for clarification?
32–43 minWeekly Planning Challenge

Use the Weekly Plan tab

Ask Isa to build a realistic seven-day plan using the items from her brain dump.

  1. Place fixed commitments first.
  2. Select the most important goals.
  3. Estimate how much time each needs.
  4. Add specific work or study blocks.
  5. Include rest, social time, and personal priorities.
  6. Leave buffer space.
  7. Explain how the plan would change if something unexpected happened.
Observe: prioritization, realistic scheduling, task specificity, balance, flexibility, and the amount of prompting needed. Do not score the beauty of the calendar; score the planning process.
43–52 minStudy-Strategy Diagnostic

Use the Study Check tab

  1. Ask Isa to study the chemistry passage using her normal method.
  2. Start the built-in four-minute timer.
  3. Hide the passage when time ends.
  4. Have her complete the recall and comprehension questions.
  5. Ask how she studied and how confident she felt.
  6. Demonstrate one quick retrieval improvement: recall first, then check.
Look for: repeated rereading, passive highlighting, copying, summarizing, self-questioning, retrieval, connecting ideas, and the relationship between confidence and accuracy.
52–57 minStudent Reflection

Use the Reflection tab

  • What did you notice about how you currently manage things?
  • Which part felt easiest?
  • Which part felt most difficult?
  • Which two skills would make the biggest difference?
  • What kind of system would you actually use?
  • What would make you stop using a system?
High-value question: What would make her stop using a system? This helps prevent building a technically strong system that she abandons.
57–60 minClose and Choose One Action

Select one small, specific action before the next session. Do not end with a large new system.

  • Complete or clean up the brain dump
  • Schedule three priority blocks
  • Test one ten-minute retrieval session
  • Record estimated versus actual time for one task
  • Choose one place to capture all current commitments
Suggested close: “We found several things you already manage well and a few places where a clearer system could save you time and stress. We are going to build the system around your actual life—not ask you to organize your life around the system.”
